AutoDesk Maya: Modeling

Easy Peasy Lemon Squeezy

For the this modeling exercise, we have to model a lemon squeezer (as shown above) using just one primitive. I thought that it was gonna be easy peezy lemon squeezy but it was not since I most experience that I had with Maya before taking this class was just deleting some objects and exporting it.

In the beginning, I was using a cylinder. However, the it creates a very pointed "squeezing part", I didn't like that and I don't know how to soften that. So, I decided to just follow that example in class and create it from a sphere.

I focused on the "squeezer part" first. This was done by deleting the bottom half of the sphere. Then, from the vertex on top of the half-sphere, I pulled it while using soft select.

After that, I selected the bottom edges and extruded it. Extending the bottom and start with the base. It looks like a star, so I translated the vertices that we still "inside", moving it outwards. Resulting to a circular base. That was not enough yet to complete the "walls." So, I extruded just a little bit more and then extruded that part towards positive-Y to create the walls.

The spout part was a little bit more difficult. I tried moving vertices and rotating it. Lastly, I selected the whole mesh and clicked on "smooth" in the modeling toolkit.
