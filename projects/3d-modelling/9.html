Principles of Animation

1. Appeal

Through out the movie, most of the characters have circular eyes that calls for positive apeal from the audience towards the characters. However, for the character that plays the role of a skeptic, calls for a negative appeal from the audience.

2. Slow-ins, Slow-outs

9, walks down the pile of books slowly and speeds up as he nears the bottom. Once 9 reached the bottom he is slowing down and compensating for the momentum.

3. Anticipation

We can anticipate that the character will be swinging his sword (which is actually a scissor blade) because we see him preparing to swing it.

4. Timing

As the character jumps up, she lifts off quickly and slows down as she reaches the maximum height of the jump and slightly speeds up as she falls. This provides the viewer a bit of information about the doll character's weight.

5. Arc

The group of characters are swinging using a rope from one ledge to another in an arc.

6. Moving Hold

The character, kept standing in one spot as the others kept moving. While he's standing he's opening and closing his eyes and also looking at his hands before cathing up with everyone.

7. Secondary Action

As the character goes into the canister, the canister also moves denoting that the canister is dynamic and reacts to the character action. Thus, giving life to a rather static object.
